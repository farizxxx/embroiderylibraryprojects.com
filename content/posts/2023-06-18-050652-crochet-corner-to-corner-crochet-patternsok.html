What is Corner to Corner Crochet?

Corner to Corner Crochet, also known as C2C, is a crochet technique that involves stitching diagonal blocks from one corner to another to create a beautiful and intricate pattern. This technique is versatile, as you can use it to create various designs, including blankets, scarves, shawls, and even garments. C2C crochet uses the basic crochet stitches, including chain, single crochet, and double crochet, to create the diagonal blocks or squares.

How to Crochet Corner to Corner

To start crocheting a corner to corner pattern, you need to know the basic crochet stitches, including chain, single crochet, and double crochet. You will also need to choose the yarn and the hook size based on the pattern you want to create. Generally, C2C patterns require a larger hook size than normal so that the stitches are loose and the fabric is soft and flexible. One of the most common ways to start a C2C crochet pattern is to create a chain of a specific length and then stitch a double crochet into it. You will then stitch single crochets into the chain until you reach the end, before turning the work. Once you have reached the other corner, you will complete the diagonal blocks by decreasing the stitches, starting with double crochet and then single crochet. You will repeat this process until you have completed the entire pattern.
