The History of Crewel Embroidery

Before we dive into the world of crewel embroidery patterns, let us take a moment to explore the history of this beautiful art form. Crewel embroidery originated in the medieval period and gained popularity in England during the Jacobean era. The name 'crewel' comes from the old French word 'crochet' which means 'a small hook.' Initially, crewel embroidery was carried out using hooks made from bones or ivory, but later, metal hooks were used. The technique involves using wool thread to create intricate designs on fabrics such as linen, silk, or cotton. The wool thread used in crewel embroidery is thicker than regular embroidery thread and is twisted much tighter. The result is a textured, elegant look that has remained popular throughout the centuries.
