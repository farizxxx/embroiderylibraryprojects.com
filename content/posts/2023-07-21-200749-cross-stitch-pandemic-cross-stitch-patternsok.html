The COVID-19 pandemic has brought about significant changes in our lives, including how we spend our time at home. With many individuals looking for new hobbies to occupy their time, cross-stitching has become a popular trend. For those interested in incorporating the pandemic into their stitching, there are various pandemic cross-stitch patterns available. In this article, we will explore some of the pandemic cross-stitch patterns that are trending for 2021.

Pandemic Cross-Stitch Patterns

One of the most popular pandemic cross-stitch patterns is the "I Survived the 2020 Pandemic" pattern from Sassi Stitch. This pattern has gained attention from stitchers all over the world, as it represents the shared experience of the pandemic. The pattern features the phrase "I Survived the 2020 Pandemic" surrounded by a frame of leaves and flowers. The finished product can be used as a wall hanging or a gift for friends and family who also survived the pandemic.

Another popular cross-stitch pattern is the "Our First Pandemic Easy Christmas 2020 Cross-Stitch" pattern from Etsy. This pattern, as the name suggests, is suitable for those who are new to cross-stitching. It features the phrase "Our First Pandemic Christmas 2020" surrounded by festive images like a wreath, snowflakes, and holly leaves. The finished product can be used as a holiday decoration, or as a gift for family and friends.

Pandemic-Themed Messages

Aside from the patterns mentioned above, there are also cross-stitch patterns that emphasize the messages and lessons we learned from the pandemic. For instance, the "Pandemic" pattern from Long Dog Samplers features an image of a virus surrounded by the words "explore, learn, innovate, endure." This pattern reminds us of the importance of staying resilient during times of crisis and the significance of coming out stronger on the other side.

Coping with COVID-19

As we continue to cope with the pandemic, it's essential to find healthy outlets for dealing with stress and anxiety. Cross-stitching has become a popular way to do this, as it promotes mindfulness and relaxation. The "Things to Do During the COVID Pandemic in 2020" pattern from Popsugar highlights some activities people have been doing during the pandemic, such as baking bread, binge-watching shows, and of course, cross-stitching. This pattern serves as a reminder to be gentle with ourselves during this challenging time and find ways to cope that work for us.

Conclusion

Overall, pandemic cross-stitch patterns serve as a way to acknowledge the shared experience of the COVID-19 pandemic while also providing a healthy outlet for stress and anxiety. The patterns mentioned in this article are just a few examples of the many designs available, and there is always the option to create a custom pattern that is unique to your experience.
