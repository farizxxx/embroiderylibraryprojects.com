What Are Granny Squares?

Granny squares are crochet motifs that are typically worked in the round from the center out. They are usually square, although they can also be rectangular or even triangular. The basic idea is to create a series of chains and double crochets that form a cluster of stitches, which is then repeated several times to create a square shape. Granny squares can be used to make all kinds of crochet projects, from blankets and bags to scarves and clothing.

How to Make Granny Squares

If you are new to crochet, making granny squares is a great way to learn some of the basic stitches. To get started, all you need is some yarn and a crochet hook. Here is a simple pattern for a traditional granny square:

Traditional Granny Square Pattern

To make a traditional granny square, follow these steps:

Step 1:

Make a magic ring and chain 3 (the first chain counts as a double crochet). Work 2 double crochets into the ring, chain 2, then work 3 more double crochets into the ring. This forms the first cluster and corner of the square.

Step 2:

Chain 2, then work 3 double crochets into the ring. This forms the second cluster and corner of the square.

Step 3:

Repeat Step 2 twice more until you have 4 clusters and corners in total, separated by chain-2 spaces.

Step 4:

Join with a slip stitch to the top of the first chain 3 to close the round.

Tips for Making Granny Squares

If you are new to making granny squares, these tips will help you get started:

1. Choose the Right Yarn

When making granny squares, it's important to choose the right type of yarn. Look for a yarn that has some give and will hold its shape well, like an acrylic or wool blend. Avoid using cotton yarn, as it can be stiff and difficult to work with.

2. Practice Your Tension

One of the most important aspects of making granny squares is tension. If your tension is too tight, your squares will be smaller than they should be. If your tension is too loose, they will be larger than they should be. Practice your tension on a small swatch of fabric before you start your project.

3. Weave in Ends Carefully

When you finish each round of your granny square, be sure to weave in the ends carefully. If you don't, the ends can come loose and your project will fall apart. Use a tapestry needle to weave in the ends through the stitches in the back of your work.
