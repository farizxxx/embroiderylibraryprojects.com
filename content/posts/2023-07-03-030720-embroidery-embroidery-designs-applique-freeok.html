Choosing the Right Fabric and Thread

When using embroidery applique designs, it's important to choose the right fabric and thread for your project. For applique, you'll need a fabric that won't fray too easily, such as felt or cotton. You'll also need a thread that matches your fabric and design. In general, it's best to choose a high-quality thread that won't break or fray easily, especially if you plan to wash your finished project.

Tips for Using Free Embroidery Applique Designs

Experiment with Different Colors

One of the best things about using embroidery applique designs is that they can be embroidered in a variety of colors. You can experiment with different color schemes to create a one-of-a-kind design that reflects your personal style. When choosing colors, it's important to consider the fabric you'll be using, as well as the intended use of the finished project.

Scale Your Design to Fit Your Project

Depending on the size of your project, you may need to scale your embroidery applique design to fit the fabric. This is especially important when embroidering designs onto clothing items, as the design may need to be scaled down to avoid looking too bulky. Most embroidery software programs allow you to easily scale your design to fit your project.

Consider Adding Embroidery Stitches

To add even more dimension and texture to your embroidery applique design, consider adding embroidery stitches around the edges of the applique. This will help prevent the fabric from fraying and give your design a more finished look. Popular embroidery stitches for applique include blanket stitch, satin stitch, and running stitch.
