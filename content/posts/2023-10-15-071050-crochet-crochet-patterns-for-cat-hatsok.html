Crochet Pattern 001 - Cute and Simple Cat Hat

This is a great crochet pattern for beginners, as it is very simple and easy to follow. The cute cat hat has two cute ears and is designed to fit most cats of any size. You can also easily personalize the hat by adding different colors or decorations.

Materials:

  • 3.5mm crochet hook
  • 50g of DK weight yarn
  • Scissors
  • Yarn needle

Instructions:

  1. Start by creating a magic circle and creating 6 single crochet (sc) stitches into it.
  2. Now, increase the stitch count to 12 by making 2 sc in each stitch.
  3. For rows 3-10, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  4. Row 11: Make 1 sc in the first stitch, 1 sc in the second stitch, then make 2 sc in the third stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  5. For rows 12-16, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  6. Row 17: Make 1 sc in the first stitch, then 2 sc in the second stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  7. For rows 18-20, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  8. Fasten off and weave in any loose ends.

Crochet Pattern 002 - Cat in the Hat

This crochet pattern is a little more complex, but it is still possible for beginners to follow. The hat is based on the popular Dr. Seuss character, Cat in the Hat. This pattern is designed for all sizes for little cats and creates an adorable look when finished.

Materials:

  • 3.5mm crochet hook
  • White, black, and red DK weight yarn
  • Scissors
  • Yarn needle

Instructions:

  1. Start by creating a magic circle and creating 6 single crochet (sc) stitches into it.
  2. Now, increase the stitch count to 12 by making 2 sc in each stitch.
  3. For rows 3-12, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  4. Row 13: Make 1 sc in the first stitch, 1 sc in the second stitch, then make 2 sc in the third stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  5. For rows 14-20, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  6. Row 21: Make 1 sc in the first stitch, then 2 sc in the second stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  7. For rows 22-25, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  8. Fasten off the white yarn and attach the black yarn. Work a round of single crochet.
  9. Row 26: With the black yarn, make 2 sc in first stitch, 1 sc in the second stitch, then make 2 sc in the third stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  10. For rows 27-29, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  11. Row 30: Make 1 sc in the first stitch, then 2 sc in the second stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  12. For rows 31-33, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  13. Fasten off the black yarn and attach the red yarn. Work a round of single crochet.
  14. Row 34: With the red yarn, make 1 sc in the first stitch, then 2 sc in the second stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  15. For rows 35-37, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  16. Fasten off and weave in any loose ends.

Crochet Pattern 003 - Flower Power Cat Hat

If you are looking for something a little more unique, then this crochet pattern for a Flower Power Cat Hat is perfect for you. The hat is designed with a fun, flowery pattern that is perfect for adding some style to your cat's wardrobe.

Materials:

  • 4mm crochet hook
  • 50g of DK weight yarn
  • Scissors
  • Yarn needle

Instructions:

  1. Start by creating a magic circle and creating 6 double crochet (dc) stitches into it.
  2. Now, increase the stitch count to 12 by making 2 dc in each stitch.
  3. For rows 3-6, make 1 dc in each stitch.
  4. Row 7: Make 1 dc in the first stitch, 1 dc in the second stitch, then make 2 dc in the third stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  5. For rows 8-10, make 1 dc in each stitch.
  6. Row 11: Make 1 dc in the first stitch, then 2 dc in the second stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  7. For rows 12-14, make 1 dc in each stitch.
  8. Fasten off and weave in any loose ends.
  9. Attach a new color of yarn and, for rows 15-20, make 1 dc in each stitch.
  10. Row 21: *Chain 2, skip a stitch, then double crochet into the next stitch*. Repeat * to * until the end of the row.
  11. For rows 22-24, continue making the flower pattern by following the instructions for row 21.
  12. Fasten off and weave in any loose ends.

Crochet Pattern 004 - Viking Cat Hat

This crochet pattern is designed for the more adventurous cat lovers out there. The Viking Cat Hat is a fun and unique way to add some personality to your cat's attire. The hat is designed with a Viking hat look, complete with horns and a furry trim.

Materials:

  • 5mm crochet hook
  • 100g of chunky weight yarn in white and brown
  • Scissors
  • Yarn needle

Instructions:

  1. Start by creating a magic circle and creating 6 single crochet (sc) stitches into it.
  2. Now, increase the stitch count to 12 by making 2 sc in each stitch.
  3. For rows 3-6, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  4. Row 7: Make 1 sc in the first stitch, 1 sc in the second stitch, then make 2 sc in the third stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  5. For rows 8-10, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  6. Row 11: Make 1 sc in the first stitch, then 2 sc in the second stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  7. For rows 12-16, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  8. Fasten off and weave in any loose ends.
  9. Attach a new color of yarn and, for rows 17-22, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  10. Row 23: With brown yarn, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  11. For rows 24-28, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  12. Row 29: Make 1 sc in the first stitch, then 2 sc in the second stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  13. For rows 30-35, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  14. Fasten off and weave in any loose ends.
  15. Create 2 horns by crocheting in the round with brown yarn. Stuff the horns with fiberfill.
  16. Sew the horns to the top of the hat.
  17. Cut strips of white yarn to create the furry trim. Sew the trim to the bottom of the hat.

Crochet Pattern 005 - Little Lady Cat Hat

This crochet pattern is designed to give your cat a more elegant and chic look. The Little Lady Cat Hat is perfect for dressing up your feline friend for a special occasion or for a day out. The hat is designed to fit most cats and features a cute bow at the top.

Materials:

  • 4mm crochet hook
  • 50g of DK weight yarn in two colors
  • Scissors
  • Yarn needle

Instructions:

  1. Start by creating a magic circle and creating 6 single crochet (sc) stitches into it.
  2. Now, increase the stitch count to 12 by making 2 sc in each stitch.
  3. For rows 3-6, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  4. Row 7: Make 1 sc in the first stitch, 1 sc in the second stitch, then make 2 sc in the third stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  5. For rows 8-10, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  6. Row 11: Make 1 sc in the first stitch, then 2 sc in the second stitch. Repeat this pattern until the end of the row.
  7. For rows 12-16, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  8. Fasten off and weave in any loose ends.
  9. Attach a new color of yarn and, for rows 17-20, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  10. Row 21: With your contrasting color, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  11. For rows 22-25, make 1 sc in each stitch.
  12. Fasten off and weave in any loose ends.
  13. Create a bow by chaining stitches in your contrasting color until the desired length is reached. Fold the bow in half and wrap yarn around the middle to create a bow shape. Sew the bow to the top of the hat.
